# South Africa Government Commitments 2026

## Justice & Safety
* Stepping up the fight against organised crime and criminal syndicates using technology, intelligence, and integrated law enforcement.
* Tackling organised crime by consolidating intelligence at national level, identifying priority syndicates, and deploying multi-disciplinary intervention teams.
* Deploying the South African National Defence Force (SANDF) to support police in dealing with gang violence and illegal mining, with tactical plans for the Western Cape and Gauteng.
* Streamlining legislation and regulations on the licensing, possession, and trading of firearms and ammunition to tackle gun crime.
* Recruiting 5,500 additional police officers this year.
* Establishing a task team to ensure swift investigations from the Madlanga Commission.
* Re-vetting Senior Management of the SAPS and Metro Police departments, including lifestyle audits by the State Security Agency.
* Establishing a new criminal justice reform initiative, modeled after Operation Vulindlela, to crack down on organised crime, corruption, and the illicit firearms trade.
* Intensifying the fight against corruption by strengthening the Special Investigating Unit, National Prosecuting Authority, and the Hawks.
* Introducing the Whistle-Blower Protection Bill in Parliament to criminalise retaliation and provide support to whistle-blowers.
* Establishing specialised courts for commercial matters with dedicated judges to prevent delays in critical projects.
* Strengthening GBVF law enforcement through faster investigation, improved case management, and expanded sexual offences courts.
* Scaling up survivor-centred support for GBVF, including access to shelters and placing Social Workers in police stations.
* Taking additional steps to secure borders through increased funding for infrastructure, technology, and personnel.
* Re-developing key border posts through public-private partnerships.
* Cracking down on violations of immigration and labour laws through joint efforts by SAPS, Home Affairs, and Labour Inspectors.
* Ensuring zero tolerance for violence or lawlessness directed at foreign nationals and protecting their access to public facilities.

## Infrastructure & Energy
* Investing over R156 billion in public funding for water and sanitation infrastructure over the next three years.
* Finalising the establishment of the National Water Resource Infrastructure Agency to manage and mobilise funding.
* Implementing the Water Services Amendment Bill to hold providers accountable, including the withdrawal of licenses for failure to deliver.
* Elevating the water crisis response to a National Water Crisis Committee chaired by the President, deploying technical experts to struggling municipalities.
* Introducing a R54 billion incentive for metros to reform their water, sanitation, and electricity services.
* Committing more than R1 trillion in public investment over three years to build and maintain general infrastructure.
* Ensuring more than 40% of energy supply comes from clean and renewable sources by 2030.
* Commencing the first round of independent transmission projects this year to enable private investment in the national grid.
* Eradicating load reduction by next year by addressing transformer overloading and illegal connections in every province.
* Initiating major public-private partnerships in port terminals and rail corridors through a concession model.
* Preparing a Request For Proposals for high-speed rail travel in South Africa.
* Investing in health infrastructure, prioritising academic hospitals starting with George Mukhari Hospital.
* Addressing the shortage of student accommodation through innovative solutions with financial institutions.
* Proceeding with Phase 4 of District Six housing construction with R500 million allocated.

## Economy & Employment
* Driving inclusive growth and job creation while reducing poverty and the high cost of living.
* Establishing a National Illicit Economy Disruption Programme targeting high-risk sectors like tobacco, fuel, and alcohol.
* Establishing a professional State Property Company to transform state-owned buildings and land into engines of growth.
* Strengthening capacity for trade negotiations and expanding missions abroad to drive economic diplomacy.
* Working with businesses and workers to close tariff loopholes and protect the manufacturing base.
* Extending the Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) system to all countries requiring a visa and all international airports/land ports, enabling 24-hour digital processing.
* Expanding support for manufacturing green products (fertiliser, jet fuel, chemicals, steel) for global markets.
* Introducing a 150% tax deduction for investment in new energy vehicles from March this year.
* Raising R2 trillion in new investments over the next five years, including hosting the sixth South Africa Investment Conference in March 2026.
* Passing the Business Licensing Bill to make it easier to start and run small businesses.
* Providing over R2.5 billion in funding to 180,000 SMEs and extending R1 billion in guarantees.
* Amending National Credit Act regulations to lower the cost of accessing credit.
* Re-aligning and strengthening the Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment (B-BBEE) framework.
* Expanding public employment programmes (CWP, EPWP, and Presidential Employment Stimulus) and improving their coordination for skills development.
* Increasing employment equity targets for persons with disabilities in the Public Service to 7% by 2030, with a matching 7% preferential procurement target.
* Hiring an additional 10,000 labour inspectors this year to tighten enforcement.
* Promoting women’s economic empowerment through training, financial support, and preferential procurement.
* Advancing regional integration and industrialisation through the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA).

## Social Welfare & Health
* Progressively realising rights to housing, health care, food, water, social security, and education.
* Expanding access to Early Childhood Development (ECD) through mass registration and increased subsidies.
* Making Grade R compulsory for all children.
* Fixing the basic education system with a focus on literacy, numeracy, and mother-tongue based bilingual education.
* Overhauling the skills development system to a dual training model and transforming the National Skills Fund.
* Increasing the skills development levy return to employers to 40% to support workplace learning.
* Developing proposals to build more universities and TVET colleges.
* Launching a mission to end child stunting by 2030 with targeted interventions for the first 1,000 days of life.
* Proposing measures to curb excessive alcohol use, including minimum unit pricing and advertising restrictions.
* Redesigning the SRD grant to better support livelihoods and work opportunities.
* Expanding affordable housing and revitalising commuter rail to reduce the cost of living.
* Implementing National Health Insurance (NHI) preparation by investing in health facilities and systems.
* Massive rollout of Lenacapavir, a six-monthly injection for preventing HIV transmission.
* Ensuring all young girls between 9 and 15 receive the HPV vaccine to end cervical cancer.
* Testing eligibility for SASSA grants remotely and providing digital social services.

## Agriculture & Environment
* Deploying 10,000 new Extension Officers to support farmers and improve productivity.
* Vaccinating the national herd of 14 million cattle against foot-and-mouth disease (FMD).
* Classifying FMD as a national disaster to mobilise state capabilities and facilitating central acquisition of vaccines.
* Establishing a task team to report monthly to the President on FMD progress.
* Dedicating funds toward geological mapping and exploration to harness critical mineral reserves.

## Governance
* Building a capable, ethical, and developmental state by strengthening anti-corruption laws and professionalising the public service.
* Finalising the approach to establishing a permanent, independent, and overarching anti-corruption body.
* Restructuring the procurement system and finalising new Public Procurement Act regulations by mid-2026.
* Implementing the Public Service Amendment Bill to protect key appointments from political interference.
* Establishing a central registry for disciplinary cases across all spheres of government to prevent the re-employment of offenders.
* Moving toward a centralised model for managing the State-Owned Enterprise (SOE) portfolio and finalising the National State Enterprises Bill.
* Laying charges against Municipal Managers in their personal capacity for violations of the National Water Act.
* Finalising a revised White Paper on Local Government to re-imagine municipal operations.
* Launching a Digital ID this year and digitising driver’s licenses, Matric certificates, and Master’s Office services.
* Making digital services available on the MyMzansi platform and expanding bank-based Smart ID/passport services.
* Advancing regional peace and stability through the AU and SADC.
* Forging partnerships with like-minded countries to increase resilience to global disruptions and launching the International Panel on Inequality.
* Continuing support for UN, AU, and SADC peacekeeping, while consolidating the Defence Force by withdrawing from the DRC.
* Conducting a National Dialogue process across all communities, culminating in a National Convention to guide the next phase of the National Development Plan.
